Home

Welcome to the Computational Neurology research group. Our mission is to leverage computational techniques to advance the understanding and treatment of neurological disorders. We specialize in using advanced machine learning algorithms on ECG and EEG data to provide insights into various neurological conditions.

Interdisciplinary

Our approach combines insights from various scientific fields to innovate and enhance neurological research and treatments.

Translational

We aim to bridge the gap between laboratory research and clinical practice, translating scientific discoveries into effective treatments.

Data oriented

Utilizing the latest advancements in technology and medicine, we strive to provide cutting-edge solutions for neurological disorders.

Projects

Data Warehousing

Using data routinely collected, we try to predict and enhance patient outcomes. In a first pilot, we predict whether patients on our stroke units have atrial fibrillation or not, from a routinely placed 1 Lead ECG.

Video Seizure Detection

We develop a platform, where parents can send short videos of their toddlers, automatically detecting whether it is a seizure or not.

Criticality

We study the behaviour of critical systems to develop a better understanding of how the brain works - and its implications for conditions like epilepsy.

UMEEG - Universal Map of EEGs

Utilizing siamese neural networks, we developed a way of encoding 10s of EEGs into a semantically meaningful 128D latent space, enabling the quantized analysis of long term EEG behaviour.

Video Monitoring

We develop a camera system that monitors patients after surgery, with the goal of recognising stroke as early as possible - greatly increasing the outcome for patients.
